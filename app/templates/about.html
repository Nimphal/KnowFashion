The idea

KnowFashion came about from a personal need of mine. Ever since I got interested in the FairTrade movement I have been paying more and more attention to where the things I buy come from. This spans all manner of items in my life, from food to technology. Clothing is just one such vein, but it happens to be a vein I am particularly interested in.

I buy pretty much 90% of my clothes online, and I am sure I am not alone. If I stick to FairTrade brands like People Tree or Komodo, then I have no qualms about the origin of my clothes. However, this limits my choice of styles severely, so I sometimes stray into the world of high street fashion. And this is where my problems start.

I am very much opposed to buying products made in China or South-East Asia. The work conditions and employer ethics there are simply not just in my mind. However, when shopping online at high street retailers, I have no way of knowing where the clothes are made. That information is not included anywhere, even in the More Info tab. My only option is to buy the product and check for myself and then return it if I'm not happy. Or...

I could try and create a place where someone who has already bought the product could share this information. And this is where KnowFashion comes. I am trying to create a place where people will want to share the origin of their clothing. I am not being political or passing judgements. I just want this information to be easily accessible online and currently it isn't.

The technology

All the code for KnowFashion is Open Source and can be found on GitHub. If you try to use it to run your own instance and it doesn't quite work, please drop me an email. I am new to this type of thing and it is very possible I forgot something.

The data from KnowFashion will also be open as per the Open Definition. Instructions about how to access it coming shortly.
